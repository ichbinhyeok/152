## Forced question
If a solo founder builds one tactical NYC compliance site in 2026, should LL152 be packaged as a checker plus filing-next-step plus LMP-routing product?

## Demand realist
- Yes.
- The trigger is sharp:
  - due now
  - filing unclear
  - penalty fear
  - need an LMP

## SERP realist
- Yes, with scope discipline.
- Generic plumber SERPs are noisy.
- Checker and filing-next-step intent is the right gap.

## Compliance realist
- Yes, but only if Follow Up #7 is built in from day one.
- No-active-gas-service and no-gas-piping must be separated every time.

## Funnel operator
- Yes.
- The CTA stack is clean:
  - check the building
  - identify the filing next step
  - route into LMP or certification help

## Sponsor seller
- Yes.
- LMP and filing-support leads are commercially plausible even at lower traffic.

## Risk operator
- Yes, with guardrails.
- The product must never pretend to provide legal certainty.
- It must never blur official filing guidance with partner claims.

## Technical architect
- Yes.
- The core logic can be expressed cleanly with file-backed rules and a checker.

## Portfolio allocator
- Approves as a `priority-2` tactical candidate.
- Stronger near-term monetization than many broad ideas, weaker long-term moat than evergreen infrastructure topics.

## Judge
- Final decision: `buildable now as a tactical 2026 wedge`
- Confidence: `8.6 / 10`

## The winning one-sentence product definition
Build an NYC LL152 checker that tells owners whether the building is due, what must be filed next, and whether they need LMP help right now.

## The biggest trap
Do not drift into generic plumbing SEO or broad district blogging.
