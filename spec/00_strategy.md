# 00 Strategy

## Positioning
Build an NYC-only LL152 checker and filing-next-step site for owners and managers who need a compliance answer now.

This is not:

- a generic NYC plumbing site
- a broad gas safety blog
- a legal-advice site
- a directory pretending to be editorial

This is:

- a compliance verdict engine
- a filing-next-step workflow product
- an LMP-routing layer

## Core wedge
The full category includes:

- applicability
- due-cycle timing
- no-gas and no-active-gas-service paths
- filing next-step logic
- extension and penalty nuance
- corrected-certification and unsafe-condition scenarios

## Phase 1 wedge
Phase 1 should be narrower than the full category.

The first wedge is:

- due-now or near-term owner intent
- checker
- filing next step
- LMP routing

If the first release does not feel like a `checker + next-step engine`, it is still too broad.

## Why this can work
- The trigger is hard and time-bound.
- Official guidance exists, but it is fragmented.
- Follow Up #7 created fresh operational nuance that many pages will miss.
- The next paid action is clear enough for lead routing.

## Strategic conclusion
The strongest product is not `What is LL152?`

It is:

`NYC LL152 checker plus filing-next-step plus LMP routing.`

## 90-day objective
Within 90 days, the site should prove three things:

1. it can earn impressions for checker and next-step routes
2. users click LMP or filing-help CTAs
3. at least a few qualified compliance leads arrive without phone-heavy operations

## Failure condition
Failure is:

- broad local plumbing traffic with no compliance intent
- pages that explain rules but do not reduce next-step ambiguity
- district content without verdict value
